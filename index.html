Emil Zatopek

Olympic distance running legend

  • 1922 - Emil Zatopek was born on September 19, in Koprivnice, Czechoslovakia. The sixth child born into his family.
  • 1944 - Zatopek breaks Czech national records in the 2,000, 3,000, and 5,000-meter competitions.
  • 1946 - Whilst still in the Army, he is selected as a member of the Czech national running team in the European Championships.
  • 1947 - Graduates from Hranice Military Academy.
  • 1948 – London Summer Olympics. Zatopek wins gold in the Men's 10,000 meters and silver in the 5,000 meters.
  • 1948 - Emil Zatopek marries Dana Zatopkova after the London Olympics. She was a javelin thrower. Some have said that one of his training techniques was to run with his wife on his back.
  • 1949 – Ostrava, Czechoslovakia. Twice breaks the world record in the Men's 10,000-meters.
  • 1950 – European Championships. Wins 5,000 and 10,000 meter races. That same year he sets the Men's 10,000-meter record in Turku, Finland
  • 1952 – Helsinki Summer Olympics. Gold medalist in 5,000 and 10,000 meter races, as well as the marathon. It was Zatopek's first ever marathon competition, and he set an Olympic record.
  • 1953 - Stará Boleslav, Czechoslovakia. Breaks Men's 10,000-meter world record
  • 1954 – European Athletics Championships. Won 10,000-meter race while becoming first athlete to beat 29 minutes. Bronze medalist in 5,000-meter race
  • 1956 – Melbourne Olympics. Finished sixth in marathon after recovering from a six-week hospitalization for a groin injury
  • 1957 - Emil Zatopek retires after his poor finish in the Melbourne Olympics.
  • 1964 - Emil Zatopek continued his career in the Army after he retired from running. He was promoted to Colonel and also held a position at the Ministry of Defense.
  • 1968 - After the Prague Spring, he was stripped of his rank, expelled from the army and removed from all important positions for his democratic beliefs. He was forced by the communist party to work in a string of menial manual labour positions.
  • 1977 - After years of working and living away from his wife and friends, Zátopek's spirit was broken and the communist government, no longer deeming him a threat, allowed him back to Prague.
  • 1998 - Awarded the title “White Lion”, one of Czech's highest awards.
  • 2000 - Emil Zatopek died on November 22, at the age of 78. His death came after a long bout of pneumonia.
  • 2000 - Awarded The Pierre de Coubertin medal from the International Olympic Committee.
  • 2012 - Inducted into the International Association of Athletics Federations Hall of Fame.
  • 2014 - Emil Zatopek was named the Greatest Runner of All Time by Runner's World Magazine with nicknames like “Czech Locomotive” and “Bouncing Czech.” A life-size statue of Zatopek was placed at the Stadium of Youth in Zlín, Czech Republic.
